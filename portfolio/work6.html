Paul Rand's Book Design

Winter 2017

Challenge: Create a book design of Paul Rand’s book, “Design and the Play Instinct”. You must choose the size of your book but it can not exceed 9x9 inches. You must include all of the text (you may not alter or remove any) and all of the images provided. You may choose from these fonts: Futura, Gill Sans, Rockwell, Helvetica, Univers. You have one restraint of choosing only one spot color, but may use black, white, and gray.

Solution: To start off, I wanted this design to be fun and engaging. I’m suppose to redesign an art book, so I wanted to fill this design with art, in combination of information and knowledge. As an art book, I strongly felt that this layout design is appropriate for the project. For the size, I chose 6x7. The reason why I chose this size is because I wanted it to be a portable book that anyone can take wherever. My inspiration was the little red book from Design 1. For titles, I chose the font Futura. I chose Futura because I wanted this book to be bold and engaging. I wanted the words to pop out with the images. For the body text, I chose Gill Sans Light. The reason why I chose Gill Sans is because it’s an elegant san-serif font that is easy to read. I wanted it to be readability and enjoyable at the same time. The color I chose was a blue- ish teal color. The reason why I chose this is because It is great color that pops. It’s mixes in well with the white. My book contains over 70 pages. For this book, I added a chapter pages to section my information. For instance, in the section “Crosswords”, the content it provides is a picture of the cross word and the text that Paul Rand’s book provides. This will give my book a great structural pattern that many books contain, so the audience doesn’t get confused as they go through the content.

Tools:
Adobe InDesign
Adobe Illustrator
Adobe Photoshop
