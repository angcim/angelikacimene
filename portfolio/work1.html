Interior Design

Fall 2017

Challenge: Create an interior design (of any room or place) by using a two-point perspective. Apply at least three different types of color schemes.

Solution: I created a two-point perspective living room space for this project. This was my first time creating an interior design. I had no idea how to create one so I looked at images on Google to provide me some inspiration. I saw many designs that included stairs, which I thought would be a fun yet challenging way to add to my interior design. For my process, I looked at many images of interior spaces and wrote a checklist on what items I want in my design (i.e. a couch, a drawer, table, etc). For the color scheme, I wanted bright and fun colors that will pop out from a mile away. The first color scheme is monochromatic. For monochromatic, I chose the color brown because first, it resembled like chocolate, and I felt that the brown interacts well with the furniture, because many furniture in real life is brown. The second color scheme is Triad. This one is my favorite out of all of them. The reason why I chose these colors is because it created a balance between warm and cool. It looks like an interior space with colors that can actually be used in real life. It gives out a 70s-80s vibe. The last two color schemes are analogous. I chose crazy and wild colors to enhance the fun and interest in this composition. Together, this composition pops out and has the audience keep looking at the small and interesting details.

Tools:
Adobe Illustrator
