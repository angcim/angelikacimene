BrainFrame

Winter 2017

Challenge: Generate an Interactive design that gives users a knowledge and information about a topic. Provide natural designs and aesthetics to create a pleasing interactive design.

Solution: For my Final in my Coding class, I created an interactive design that navigates the users through different parts of the brain. Through research, we chose five main important parts of the brain. The objective of our design was to represent the interaction between the music and the brain as we show it with aesthetic patterns and visuals. As the viewer hovers through the boxes in the corner (through mouseHover), the visuals will appear with music that correspond to image. In order to achieve this, we downloaded libraries such as gifAnimation and a minim player. In the corner of the interface is a photo of the brain and when each part is clicked (by using the mouseClick function), the person is able to read what that part of the brain does and why itâ€™s significant. The reason for this function so that the audience will be able to understand what each part of the brain does. As a person who knows nothing about the functions of the brain, we believed that it would be a helpful tool to know how each part operates. In the corner of the animation, the audience can press 1,2,3,4,5. This will add more patterns and enhance the experience of the user. This was added in our design because the goal of this was to show information in an engaging and visually pleasing way.
